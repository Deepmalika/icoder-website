üíæ Ransomware Protection
Ransomware is a major threat. Learn how to safeguard your data from encryption-based attacks.

The most common types of cyberattacks include:

Phishing: A technique where attackers deceive users into disclosing sensitive information via fake emails or websites.

Malware: Software designed to cause harm, such as viruses, worms, and ransomware.

Ransomware: A type of malware that locks access to a system or data and demands a ransom for release.

Denial-of-Service (DoS): An attack that floods a system, network, or server with traffic to disrupt its operations.

Man-in-the-Middle (MitM): An attack where the attacker intercepts communication between two parties to steal data.

To protect your personal data online:

Use strong, unique passwords for each account.

Enable multi-factor authentication (MFA) wherever possible.

Avoid clicking on links or downloading attachments from unknown emails or websites.

Keep your software and operating system updated to patch vulnerabilities.

Use a reputable antivirus and anti-malware solution.

Be cautious when sharing personal information on social media or websites.

If your account is hacked, take these steps:

Change your password immediately and enable two-factor authentication (2FA) if possible.

Review your account activity for any unauthorized actions.

Notify the service provider or website about the breach.

Check your other accounts for any signs of unauthorized access.

If sensitive information (e.g., banking details) was compromised, consider freezing your accounts or notifying relevant authorities.

Public Wi-Fi networks, such as those in coffee shops or airports, are typically unsecured, meaning:

They can be targeted by hackers to intercept your data.

Attackers may set up rogue Wi-Fi hotspots to steal sensitive information.

Your personal information (e.g., passwords, credit card details) is at risk when transmitted over an unencrypted network.
